Public sector fit-out and alteration project for the Legal Aid Board’s Smithfield Law Centre. The project involved the phased refurbishment of an existing multi-storey building to upgrade public-facing law centre accommodation, office areas, meeting rooms, staff facilities, support spaces, services infrastructure and circulation areas.

As Architect I was involved from sketch design through tender, construction and handover. The works were delivered across multiple phases, including upper floor renovations, roof-level works, ground floor reconfiguration, staircore works and basement/plant-related items. The building remained in use during the project, requiring careful phasing, staff relocation planning, access coordination, escape route coordination, landlord/building management coordination and continuous consultant input.

The project was delivered under a Public Works Contract. My role focused on architectural design coordination, tender and construction documentation, construction-stage drawing updates, architectural responses to site queries, site inspections and snagging.
